Embekke Devalaya

Embekke Devalaya is a historic temple located in Sri Lanka. It is situated in the village of Embekke, near the town of Kandy. The temple is renowned for its intricate woodcarvings, which are considered to be some of the finest examples of traditional Sinhalese craftsmanship.

The Embekke Devalaya was built during the reign of King Vickramabahu III in the 14th century. It was originally dedicated to the worship of the Hindu deity Kataragama, but over time, it also became a place of Buddhist worship. The temple is part of the Gampola era of Sinhalese architecture.

The main highlight of Embekke Devalaya is its wooden pillars, which are intricately carved with various motifs and designs. These carvings depict a wide range of subjects, including dancers, musicians, birds, animals, and mythological figures. The craftsmanship and attention to detail in these carvings are truly remarkable.

Embekke Devalaya is not only a place of religious significance but also a popular tourist attraction. Visitors can admire the intricate woodwork and experience the cultural and architectural heritage of Sri Lanka. It is recommended to visit the temple with a guide who can provide insights into its history and significance.
