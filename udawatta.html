Udawatta Kele Sanctuary

The Udawatta Kele Sanctuary, also known as the Royal Forest Park, is a beautiful nature reserve located in Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is a protected forest area situated on a hill ridge overlooking the city of Kandy and covering an area of about 103 hectares.

The Udawatta Kele Sanctuary is known for its rich biodiversity and natural beauty. It serves as an important habitat for various species of flora and fauna, including endemic and endangered species. The sanctuary is home to numerous bird species, butterflies, reptiles, and mammals such as monkeys, deer, and porcupines.

Within the sanctuary, you'll find several walking trails and pathways that allow visitors to explore the lush greenery, ancient trees, and serene atmosphere. There are also several small ponds and streams that add to the charm of the place. The sanctuary is known for its peaceful ambiance and is often considered a tranquil retreat away from the bustling city.

n addition to its natural beauty, the Udawatta Kele Sanctuary holds historical and cultural significance as well. It was once a part of the royal palace complex of the Kandyan Kingdom and served as a private garden for the Kandyan kings. The sanctuary is dotted with ancient structures, including meditation caves, shrines, and hermitages, some of which date back to the 3rd century BC.
