Ambuluwawa Tower

The Ambuluwawa Tower, also known as Ambuluwawa Biodiversity Complex, is a unique and iconic tower located in Gampola, Sri Lanka. It is a multi-purpose structure that serves as a communication tower, observation deck, and a symbol of environmental conservation.

The Ambuluwawa Tower stands at a height of 48 meters (157 feet) and is situated on top of the Ambuluwawa Mountain, which is approximately 1,100 meters (3,600 feet) above sea level. The tower offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including mountains, valleys, and lush greenery.

One of the key features of the Ambuluwawa Tower is its unique architectural design. The structure is inspired by the shape of a stupa, a sacred Buddhist monument, and incorporates elements of various world religions, representing the country's religious harmony. The tower's construction was initiated by the Sri Lanka Institute of Architects as a tribute to the late Deshamanya Geoffrey Bawa, a renowned Sri Lankan architect.

The Ambuluwawa Biodiversity Complex is not only a tourist attraction but also serves as a center for biodiversity conservation and environmental education. The complex includes a biodiversity park with various plant species, a meditation center, a herbal garden, and an educational center. Visitors can explore the complex, learn about Sri Lanka's diverse flora and fauna, and enjoy the serene surroundings.
