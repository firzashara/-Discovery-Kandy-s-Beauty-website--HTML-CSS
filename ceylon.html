Ceylon Tea Museum

The Ceylon Tea Museum in Kandy is a popular tourist attraction located in the Hantane region, just a few kilometers from the city center of Kandy, Sri Lanka. The museum is housed in the former Hanthana Tea Factory, which dates back to the 1920s. It offers visitors an insightful journey through the history and production process of Ceylon tea.

The museum is situated in a picturesque tea estate and provides a comprehensive overview of the history of tea production in Sri Lanka. It showcases the evolution of tea cultivation from the colonial era to the present day.

The museum exhibits a range of artifacts, machinery, photographs, and historical documents related to the tea industry. Visitors can learn about the origins of tea in Sri Lanka, the different tea-growing regions, and the impact of the industry on the country's economy.

The Ceylon Tea Museum is situated amidst lush greenery, surrounded by tea plantations and panoramic views of the Kandy landscape. The serene environment provides a tranquil setting for visitors to relax and enjoy their tea experience. Visiting the Ceylon Tea Museum in Kandy is a great way to learn about the rich heritage and culture of tea in Sri Lanka. It's an educational and enjoyable experience for tea enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone interested in exploring Sri Lanka's tea industry.
